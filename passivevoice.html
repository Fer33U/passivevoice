Passive voice

Simple present

Use of is or are depending if the subject is singular or plural
The structure would be

subject + verb to be (is/are) + passive participle

Question
Which one is correct?
A) Spanish is spoken in Mexico
B)The tv was broken by him

Question
Which one is correct?
A) The car was stolen
B) The letter is written by Tom

Past continuous

Use of was or were depending if the subject is singular or plural
The structure would be

subject + verb to be (was/were) + verb ing + complement

Question
Which one is correct?
A)The house will be sold by the owners next month
B)The window was being repaired by the carpenter

Question
Which one is correct?
A) The car was being fixed by the mechanic
B) The report will be submitted by the team on Friday

Presente perfecto

Use of has/have been
The structure would be

subject + have/has been + participle verb + complement

Question
Which one is correct?
A)The room has been cleaned by me
B)I'm Kevin

Question
Which one is correct?
A) She reads books every evening
B) The project has been finished by us

Futuro simple

Use of will or shall
The structure would be

subject + will/shall be + past participle verb + complement

Question
Which one is correct?
A)I make a cupcake today
B)The new rules shall be implemented next month

Question
Which one is correct?
A) The letters are delivered by the mail carrier every morning
B) The seeds will be planted in the garden next spring

Modal verbs

Use of will or shall
The structure would be

subject + modal + be + verb (past participle) + complement

Question
Which one is correct?
A)The document ought to be reviewed before signing
B)The door should be locked at all times

Question
Which one is correct?
A) The message can be sent by email
B) I walk to work every morning
